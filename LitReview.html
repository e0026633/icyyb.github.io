Literature Review

Our literature review mainly consists of looking at studies on heat vulnerability in other areas as well as Yangon’s current heat mitigation efforts and studies, in order to identify key research gaps which will guide our study.

Through studies such as Inostroza et. al. (2016), Johnson et. al. (2012), Reid et. al. (2009), and Huang et. al. (2011), we identified that heat vulnerability analysis takes into account environmental variables (e.g. land surface temperature), and socioeconomic variables related to heat risk (e.g. income levels, housing type, race distribution, etc.). From these papers, we also adapted the methodologies relevant for our study.

In order to design a study that is relevant to Yangon, we looked at the Yangon’s urban development plans and other heat mitigation related studies contextualised in Yangon. It was found that heat-stress was not mentioned in development plans (Yangon City Development Committee, 2014). Additionally, there are limited heat-related studies of Yangon, with only one study on drought resilience. As such, it is observed that research is yet to be done on Yangon’s heat-stress vulnerability, which will be the focus of our study.
