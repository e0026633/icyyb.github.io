Introduction

With Myanmar having recently opened its economy to the world, its capital Yangon is undergoing development and urbanisation at an unprecedented rate and this trend is expected to persist. Situated in the tropics, Yangon also has to manage potential climate risks that increasing global temperatures and climate change bring, such as their effect on public health, as heat-stress related illnesses have been on a continual rise (WHO, 2018).

Yangonâ€™s unique position as an emerging region in a tropical climate makes it an interesting area of study. As there is a lack in heat-stress vulnerability research on Yangon, our study serves as a starting point to better understand the spatial distribution of heat-stress areas within Yangon. Using GIS analysis and adapting methods such as Analytical Hierarchy Process, the study couples land surface temperature data with social variables to generate a township-based heat-stress vulnerability map for Yangon. As vulnerability is a function of physical and social factors, we hypothesise that rural areas in Yangon will have higher heat-stress vulnerability as these localities lack the capacity to mitigate high heat exposure. Identifying these heat-vulnerable townships can provide local authorities with useful information for mitigation strategies.
